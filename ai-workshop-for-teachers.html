What Exactly Are We Talking About?

When we say "AI tools for teachers," we're talking about Large Language Models (LLMs) like ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini‚Äîextremely well-read assistants who've processed billions of documents.

üí°
Key insight: These tools predict what text should come next based on patterns. This is why they can be brilliantly helpful and confidently wrong.

What AI Is Good At

Generating first drafts, brainstorming, explaining concepts differently, transforming text (summarizing, expanding, translating), creating variations, handling repetitive writing tasks.

What AI Struggles With

Precise factual accuracy, math calculations, original creative insight, understanding your specific students, making professional judgments requiring your context.

üìö Learn More: How LLMs Work‚ñº

LLMs learn statistical patterns about which words tend to appear together. When you ask a question, they generate plausible-sounding text rather than checking facts‚Äîwhich is why they "hallucinate."

What NOT to Use AI For

‚ö†Ô∏è
Golden Rule: Never input student names or personal data into public AI tools.

Avoid AI For:

  • Final fact-checking ‚Äì Always verify facts independently
  • Student data ‚Äì GDPR/DSGVO applies
  • Calculations ‚Äì AI is bad at math
  • Current events ‚Äì Knowledge cutoffs apply
üá©üá™ German Legal Framework‚ñº
